Dispersal



Flight mill. We continue updating this machine using laser cutting and 3D printing techniques. 3D printers and laser cutters are available at the MADD Center at UChicago.


Given how intractable the dispersal of insects is in the field, we use a flight mill to address an important ecological phenomenon – how insects move. See this preprint to read up more on our design and construction.


We also explore how landscape structure influences dispersal evolution using theoretical simulations.

Why do insects disperse?


Individual movement determines where and how ecological interactions happen, what evolutionary pressures populations experience, and the extent of gene flow possible between disparate populations. We study how genetic and plastic differences between individuals influence their flight capacity using field and lab experiments.





Average speed of soapberry bugs across latitude in Florida, US. We also calculate additional flight statistics such as max speed, distance, duration, and periodicity. All results are automatedly collected and calculated through Python scripts.
