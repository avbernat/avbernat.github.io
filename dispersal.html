Dispersal



Flight mill. We continue updating this machine using laser cutting and 3D printing techniques. 3D printers and laser cutters are available at the MADD Center at UChicago.



We use a flight mill to address an important ecological phenomenon – how insects move. This design allows us to observe individual flight propensity, speed, distance, repeatability, and periodicity under controlled environmental conditions. See this preprint from Anastasia Bernat to read more about the design and construction of this flight mill and automated Python scripts for processing flight recordings.


We explore how landscape structure influences dispersal and diapause evolution using theoretical simulations built in R.

Why do insects disperse?


Individual movement determines where and how ecological interactions happen, what evolutionary pressures populations experience, and the extent of gene flow possible between disparate populations. We study how genetic and plastic differences between individuals influence their flight capacity using field and lab experiments and theoretical simulations.
