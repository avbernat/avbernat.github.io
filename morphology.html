Morphology

What is their physical form?


The soapberry bug has a particular evolutionary history. As a plant-feeding specialist, it rapidly adapted to feed on the introduced genus of plants in its range in North America. In the 1980s, populations of the red-shouldered soapberry bug (Jadera haematoloma) locally adapted to feed on the seeds of a native host plant, Halloon vine, and an introduced host plant, golden rain tree. By 2014, local differentiation in beak length had been lost, likely as a consequence of increased gene flow.


We use microscopic photography to measure the evolving physical form of soapberry bugs. We take body length, wing length, thorax length, beak length, and wing-area measurements. In addition, we conduct dissections under the microscope for wing-muscle digestion.
