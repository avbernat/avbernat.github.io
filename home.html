We research how the barriers between locally adapted populations and young species respond, both genetically and via plasticity, to different environments. We use field- and lab-based experiments, observations, and theory.


We value and practice intellectual integrity, collaboration, and diversity of thought. We behave respectfully, professionally, and safely to all lab members regardless of race, identity, class, gender, or sexual orientation. Our lab culture is one not only centered on making bold commitments to ecological discovery but it also questions the ethical and political institutions that structurally uphold our field. We believe research is a powerful tool that connects and contextualizes us in our world. In turn, we acknowledge our responsibility for our actions and seek to give others our discoveries.


We thank our institutions and sponsors who make our research possible.
