Reproductive Plasticity

How do they choose mates?


In sexually reproducing species, choosing a mate is one of the most crucial decisions determining an individualâ€™s fitness. These decisions also determine when differentiation and gene flow between populations are possible. We study how insect species developing in and adapting to novel environments influences mating behavior.


We do this by conducting mate trials. We raise the juveniles of field-collected bugs and then house two males (each from a different host plant) and one female in a large bug home. During mate trials, we count male-female mounting events throughout each day and for most of their adult life.





Small bug homes. Each bug home houses an individual juvenile as they grow.
