Can we see evolution on human timescales?


Long-term ecological and evolutionary studies are critical for understanding how biological systems respond to ongoing environmental change. In this system, we have already seen the emergence and collapse of local adaptation over the course of a few short decades. We conduct repeated observations to understand patterns of phenotypic and genetic change over seasonal, annual, and decadal timescales.


We map host plant distributions through time using herbarium records. These mappings help us witness the emergence and collapse of local adaption over the course of a few short decades. to map historic geographic distributions and compare to present-day distributions.
