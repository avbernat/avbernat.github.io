Host Plant Ecology

What happens to the native community?


When organisms adapt to novel environments, this does not happen in a vacuum. We study how genes flow back to populations due to ancestral and environmental impacts on a species' ability to persist and compete. These impacts, in turn, shift the evolutionary pressure organisms exert in the native community.


We are currently growing the native host plant of the soapberry bug, balloon vine, in hopes of stocking up on its seeds. Additionally, we conduct field experiments to test seed damage across different host plants.





Balloon vine saplings. We grow host plants at the Greenhouse in the Biological Sciences Learning Center (BSLC).


Hand-made mesh bags used to enclose Balloon Vine seed pods and keep out bugs. We are testing to see how seed damage varies across different host plants. This is our control unit.
